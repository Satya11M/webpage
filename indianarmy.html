Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India serves as the Supreme Commander of the Indian Army, and it is commanded by the Chief of Army Staff (COAS), who is a four-star general.

History

The Indian Army traces its roots back to the British Indian Army, which was established in 1895. The Indian Army has played a major role in multiple conflicts and operations, including World War II, the Indian independence movement, and the Indo-Pakistani Wars.

Structure

The Indian Army is divided into seven commands, each headed by a Lieutenant General. These commands are responsible for different geographic regions and are responsible for the overall defense of the country.

Ranks and Insignia

The Indian Army has a hierarchical rank structure, with different ranks and insignia for officers and enlisted personnel. The highest rank in the Indian Army is Field Marshal, which is a ceremonial rank and is only held by a select few.
